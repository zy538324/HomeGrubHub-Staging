What is Meal Planning?

Meal planning is the simple practice of deciding your meals in advance - typically for a week at a time. Instead of asking "what's for dinner?" every evening, you already know exactly what you're cooking.

For UK families, meal planning typically involves:

  • Planning 7 days of meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks)
  • Creating a shopping list based on planned meals
  • Shopping once per week with your list
  • Prepping ingredients where possible

Getting Started: Your First Week

Start Simple

Don't try to plan every meal perfectly in your first week. Start with just planning dinners, and add breakfast/lunch later once you're comfortable.

What You'll Need:

  • 30 minutes of planning time (usually Sunday works best)
  • A meal planning template (get our free one below)
  • Access to recipes (cookbooks, websites, or apps like HomeGrubHub)
  • Your family's schedule for the week

Step-by-Step Meal Planning Process

1

Check Your Schedule

Look at your week ahead. When will you be home for dinner? Any late meetings? Kids' activities? Plan easier meals for busy days.

2

Choose Your Recipes

Select 7 dinner recipes. Mix familiar favorites with 1-2 new dishes. Consider prep time, ingredients you already have, and family preferences.

3

Create Shopping List

List all ingredients needed. Check what you already have at home. Organize by supermarket sections to shop efficiently.

4

Shop Smart

Stick to your list! Check for deals on your planned ingredients. Consider buying extra of non-perishables when on offer.

Money-Saving Meal Planning Tips for UK Families

Plan meals around what's in season. August means cheap tomatoes, courgettes, and sweetcorn. Winter calls for root vegetables and hearty stews. Seasonal produce is always cheaper and tastes better.

Tesco Clubcard, Sainsbury's Nectar, ASDA Rewards - they all offer genuine savings. Plan meals around your store's weekly offers and earn points on your regular shopping.

Make double portions of soups, casseroles, and curries. Freeze half for later weeks. This saves money on ingredients and gives you ready meals for busy nights.
